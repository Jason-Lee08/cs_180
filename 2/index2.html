Part 1: Gradient Magnitude Computation

Gradient magnitude computation is an essential technique in image processing, often used for detecting edges by calculating the magnitude of the image gradient. This method highlights areas of high intensity change, which usually correspond to the boundaries of objects in the image. The gradient magnitude is derived using partial derivatives of the image intensity values, typically calculated via convolution with Sobel or Prewitt filters.
